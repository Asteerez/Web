The goal of this webpage is dedicated to explaining how caffeine consumption affects mental health and wellbeing, going over specifics and fun facts

Caffeine is the most widely consumed stimulant in the world, though its effects on mental health remain incompletely understood.

Key Findings ✨

Caffeine and the Brain

Caffeine is a psychoactive drug that is absorbed into the body and then into the brain where it interferes with adenosine receptors, a sleep chemical in the brain, halting its effects that would typically make you drowsy.

Caffeine affects nerve tissue present in all of the body, meaning that your whole body feels the affects of caffeine. However, it reaches the brain much more quickly than other kinds of nerve tissues. Caffeine does not actually give you energy, it only stimulates your brain. Excess amounts of caffeine can cause convulsions or muscle twitchings, typically around 10 grams of caffeine or more, which is equivalent to drinking 70 cups of coffee.

Caffeine and the body

Caffeine has been known to cause certain digestive activities as a result of it's diuretic properties however, did you also know that caffeine increases the effectiveness of sugar?

The body can build tolerance to caffeine, like any drug, large amounts over extended periods of time will build tolerance to caffeine typically

Caffeine increases effectiveness of glucose which is why you can notice feelings of:

  • Heightened Energy
  • increased mental awareness
  • better memory retention
  • possible decrease of risk to chronic diseases

Caffeine Withdrawal

The brain can become addicted to caffeine, only taking around 100mg to become addicted to caffeine, product containing caffeine also contain sugar, which boost dopamine in the brain, increasing it's addictive properties. This is why, it's recommended that Pregnant women consume less caffeine, because a developing fetus can be affected strongly by the caffeine in the mother's blood

Caffeine withdrawal is recognized by the world health organization as a mental disorder. Caffeine withdrawal has been identified to cause flu like symptoms and requires trivial quantities of caffeine to incite.

Studies from John Hopkins University regarding the effects of caffeine withdrawal concluded 50% of groups exposed to caffeine consumption that have had their caffeine intake removed experienced headaches and negative moods whilst 13% said that they had physically felt ill.

Futhermore, the effects of caffeine withdrawal were noticed after only 12 hours of caffeine isolation.

interestingly, doctors and physicians alike still have no framework that diagnoses caffeine withdrawal, despite it's acknolwedged existence

Caffeine and YOU ✨

Caffeine consumption has been known overtime to decrease the likelihood of depressive symptoms, though haven't been sufficiently studied.

Caffeine can also induce manic and psychotic symptoms in people, also increasing anxiety.

People with anxiety, or panic disorders are especially sensitive to the anixogenic-anxiety inducing effects of caffeine. However, people with OCD seem to benefit from caffeine, expeiencing less negative drawbacks than others.

Furthermore, caffeine's interruption of the sleep cycle when improperly consumed can lead to negative mental health attributes by a matter of proxy.

The benefits of caffeine and its consumption should also be understood that it is a stimulant that can create withdrawal effects in the brain as stated prior. The withdrawal affects can be adverse and considered drawbacks of the mental health benefits that can be received from consumption.

Considerations

Caffeine hasn't been sufficiently studied with proper experiments. Leaving a lack of objective evidence outside of its prestudied function and anectdotal proofs.

Despite the fact caffeine is consumed in nearly every household in the united states it remains one of the least understood drugs.

however, from the existing data we can determine that the effects of caffeine on mental health are invariable but have a slight positive correlation when consumed responsibly. Otherwise, caffeine can be considered a detriment to wellbeing and mental health if abused
